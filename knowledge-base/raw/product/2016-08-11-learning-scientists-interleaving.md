# Learn To Study Using…Interleaving

- Source: http://www.learningscientists.org/blog/2016/8/11-1
- Collected: 2026-05-26
- Published: 2016-08-11

## Excerpts

Definition excerpt:

"Today’s post is about interleaving – that is, switching between ideas while you study."

"You shouldn’t study one idea, topic, or type of problem for too long. Instead, you should change it up often."

Benefits excerpt:

Interleaving can help learners:

- "choose the correct strategy to solve a problem"
- "see the links, similarities, and differences between ideas"

Constraint excerpt:

"While it’s good to switch between ideas, don’t switch too often"

The article recommends using interleaving together with retrieval practice or elaboration rather than passive rereading.

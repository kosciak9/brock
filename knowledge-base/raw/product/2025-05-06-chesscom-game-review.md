# Chess.com Game Review

- Source: https://support.chess.com/en/articles/8584089-how-does-game-review-work
- Collected: 2026-05-26
- Published: 2025-05-06

## Excerpts

After a game, Chess.com offers a "Game Review" flow with a summary screen and a review button immediately on the end-game UI.

Highlights excerpt:

- "Game Graph: This graph indicates who had the advantage at each stage of the game."
- "Accuracy: This score, ranging from 0 to 100, represents how accurately you played according to the engine’s evaluations."

Move classification excerpt:

The review labels moves as "Brilliant", "Great", "Best", "Excellent", "Good", "Book", "Inaccuracy", "Mistake", "Miss", and "Blunder".

Navigation/review excerpts:

- "Click 'Next' to review the key moves in the game."
- "A key move ... refers to a critical move during a game that significantly influences the outcome or course of the match."
- "If a key move could have been better, you’ll have the chance to 'Retry' the move."
- Users can "see the computer's move predictions" and "review the game from your opponent's perspective".

# Learn how to Study Using... Retrieval Practice

- Source: http://www.learningscientists.org/blog/2016/6/23-1
- Collected: 2026-05-26
- Published: 2016-06-23

## Excerpts

Definition excerpt:

"Retrieval practice involves recreating something you’ve learned in the past from your memory, and thinking about it right now."

Value excerpt:

"Compared to simply studying by looking over your notes, if you practice retrieval you’re more likely to remember the information later, and also more likely to be able to use and apply the information in new situations."

Suggested retrieval methods from the article:

- practice tests or practice questions
- make your own questions
- flashcards used without peeking
- write down everything you know on a topic
- draw everything you know from memory
- concept maps made from memory first

Feedback-loop excerpt:

"After practicing retrieval, it is best to then check your book and notes ... This process corrects any misunderstandings, and gives you feedback about what you know and what you don’t know."
